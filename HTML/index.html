    • About Me

    Hello world!

    My name is Mohnish Pai, and I'm a computer engineering student at Texas A&M University. I've written code in C++, HTML, Python, and Verilog, and I've done research in semiconductor electrochemistry, ASIC placement and routing. My research interests also involve circuit design and neural networks. I like playing and modifying video games, cooking, watching YouTube, rock climbing, and dabbling with machine learning projects in my spare time.

    My interest in computers began at an early age. When I was four years old, I lived with my grandparents in India and had access to a PC that ran Windows XP. My father left the computer there since he was working in the US at the time, allowing me to tinker with it. I recall spending hours on it playing 3D Space Cadet Pinball and watching Tom and Jerry cartoons, and that experience cemented my passion for and immersion in the world of computing.

    Moving on to high school, my love for games would drive me to study more about the hardware that powered them. I'd obsessively investigate cutting-edge CPUs and how companies could obtain incremental performance increases over time. Furthermore, cryptocurrency mining became profitable around the same time, reinforcing my desire to delve deeper into ASIC design.

    When I enrolled in the Texas Academy of Math and Science high school program, I was able to gain a more in-depth understanding of semiconductor production. I conducted undergraduate research on copper etching methods in order to develop a regeneration cupric chloride solution for recurrent usage in etching batches. My experience learning about the chip manufacturing process led me to study more about digital design, which is what I do now at Texas A&M. I have worked on projects using ML models in VLSI circuit design and neural network chip design with Dr. Jiang Hu.

    Fourier Transform Crypto Trader

    An implementation of a deep neural network processor using Verilog, Pytorch, Python, and C++. Designed for a regularity-based flow to achieve the highest throughput and lowest power consumption.

    A fork of the Freqtrade cryptocurrency trading platform that adds a trading algorithm based on the Fourier transform. Takes real-time and historical data from the price history of a cryptocurrency, applies FFT to the signal, and trades at regular intervals using the sine wave of the largest magnitude.

    Service

    As Undergraduate Director of the research volunteering organization Scope Research, I lead undergraduate research students to guide high school students through the research process. Our team of 20+ undergraduate and graduate students throughout A&M volunteered as mentors to guide high school students in their pursuit of academic research.

    I interviewed multiple candidates to make sure that they understood our process and were interested in contributing to our goal. By constructing a set of research guidelines, I provided the undergraduate mentors with a standardized format and an end goal for them to reach with their high school mentees. Our team collaborated with more than 30 A&M research organizations to introduce more students to the research and mentorship process.
